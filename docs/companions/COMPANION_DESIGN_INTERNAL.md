# Companion Narrative Design (Spoiler-Heavy)

## 1) Isolde vex-Torvath

### Backstory
Isolde is the eldest daughter of the Torvath military dynasty. She was raised to embody Core values: honor, duty, law. But she harbors a secret - her father ordered a massacre she witnessed as a child and was told to forget. She's seeking proof to either vindicate or condemn her bloodline.

### Personal Quest: "The Torvath Record"
The player discovers the massacre records. Isolde must choose:

**Good Path (Honor):** Confront her father publicly, accept family disgrace, become a true champion of justice.

**Evil Path (Pragmatism):** Destroy the records, protect the family name, become an enforcer who "deals with problems."

**Quest Subversion:** The massacre victims were actually corrupt officials about to expose her father's embezzlement. Was he right? Was she wrong to judge?

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Redeemer:** Becomes a legendary reformer who tears down corrupt institutions.
- **Zealot Inquisitor:** Becomes a terrifying enforcer who "protects the Core" through fear.

### Breaking Points
- Player commits treason against the Core
- Player executes surrendering enemies
- Player betrays Isolde personally

## 2) Bram "The Vulture"

### Backstory
Bram grew up in the Sump's orphan gutters. He survived by stealing, cheating, and occasionally killing. He's not proud - he's pragmatic. His "Vulture" moniker comes from his habit of appearing after battles to loot the dead. But there's more: he's been secretly funding an orphanage.

### Personal Quest: "The Vulture's Heart"
Bram's former crew wants him back. They're planning a major heist. He must choose:

**Good Path (Compassion):** Turn informant, help the guards, destroy the criminal network.

**Evil Path (Pragmatism):** Join the heist, betray the guards, become the new crime boss.

**Quest Subversion:** The heist targets a corrupt merchant's vault - the same one who runs the orphanages. Stealing from him might save more orphans than turning informant.

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Spymaster:** Uses his network for good, becomes an information broker for the powerless.
- **Contractor:** Neutral mercenary, works for anyone who pays.

### Breaking Points
- Player kills an unarmed enemy
- Player steals from innocents
- Player refuses to help orphans

## 3) Dr. Sybilla ves-Vael

### Backstory
Sybilla was a prodigy at the Academy, obsessed with forbidden knowledge. She discovered the "true history" - that the Core's founding was built on a massive lie. She couldn't prove it. The Academy silenced her research and reassigned her. She's seeking proof to expose the truth.

### Personal Quest: "The Chronicler's Burden"
Sybilla finds the original founding documents. She must choose:

**Good Path (Truth):** Publish everything, accept the consequences, rebuild from truth.

**Evil Path (Ambition):** Use the knowledge as blackmail, rise through the ranks, become the new power behind the throne.

**Quest Subversion:** The documents reveal that *every* region was founded on lies. Is exposing one lie worth destabilizing all of civilization?

### Transformation Outcomes
- **True Healer:** Focuses on healing rather than knowledge, becomes a compassionate sage.
- **Blood Alchemist:** Turns forbidden knowledge into dark power, becomes dangerous.

### Breaking Points
- Player lies to Sybilla
- Player destroys knowledge (even corrupted)
- Player threatens her research

## 4) Garrick nul-Kael

### Backstory
Garrick was a decorated general who refused orders to massacre civilians. He was stripped of rank and exiled. Now he wanders, seeking to prove that honor and pragmatism can coexist. He's tired of watching good soldiers follow bad orders.

### Personal Quest: "The Last Command"
Garrick's former soldiers are deserting and need leadership. He must choose:

**Good Path (Service):** Lead them to defend a village, become a liberator.

**Evil Path (Power):** Use them to overthrow the corrupt government, become a warlord.

**Quest Subversion:** The "corrupt government" is the only thing keeping a worse faction from taking over. Garrick must decide if imperfect order is better than chaos.

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Shield-Captain:** Leads armies to protect the innocent.
- **Reaver:** Becomes a feared conqueror.

### Breaking Points
- Player orders Garrick to attack civilians
- Player abandons his men
- Player takes the easy path over the right one

## 5) Oona Pale-Sap

### Backstory
Oona is bound to a tree spirit - literally. Her body is half-plant. She's the last of the "merged" - those who perfectly bonded with the Choke's nature. She seeks to either restore the old ways or accept the changing world.

### Personal Quest: "Root and Reach"
The forest is dying. Oona must choose:

**Good Path (Balance):** Find a way to heal without destroying human settlements.

**Evil Path (Nature):** Drive out the humans, let nature reclaim everything.

**Quest Subversion:** The "dying forest" is actually awakening into something new and more powerful. Is change always destruction?

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Guardian of Growth:** Becomes a protector of both nature and civilization.
- **Blight-Touched:** Becomes a corrupted force of nature.

### Breaking Points
- Player destroys natural sites
- Player refuses to help the forest
- Player treats nature as a resource

## 6) Balance-Ninth (Theron)

### Backstory
Theron was once a street criminal named Keth. He fled to Zenith seeking meaning. The monks saw potential in his darkness and taught him to find balance. He seeks to prove that anyone can transcend their past.

### Personal Quest: "The Final Test"
Theron's old gang finds him. They want him back. He must choose:

**Good Path (Transcendence):** Help his old friends find meaning at Zenith.

**Evil Path (Rejection):** Destroy his past entirely, embrace the monk life completely.

**Quest Subversion:** The gang's leader is actually a fallen monk who was expelled for embracing darkness. Theron must face the teacher who failed.

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Bodhisattva:** Achieves enlightenment, helps others transcend.
- **Void Walker:** Embraces the void, becomes something otherworldly.

### Breaking Points
- Player forces Theron to abandon his beliefs
- Player commits violence against innocents
- Player rejects Theron's help

## 7) Sash "Velvet"

### Backstory
Sash has a hundred identities and no real name. She's a spy, con artist, and information broker. But there's a core: she was betrayed by someone she loved, and now she trusts no one. She stays with the player out of curiosity and perhaps a growing attachment.

### Personal Quest: "The Velvet Mask"
Sash's old network wants her back - or dead. She must choose:

**Good Path (Trust):** Let the player help her dismantle the network.

**Evil Path (Control):** Take over the network, become untouchable.

**Quest Subversion:** The network's leader is Sash's mother, who abandoned her. Revenge or reconciliation?

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Selfless Spy:** Uses skills to protect those who can't protect themselves.
- **Velvet Assassin:** Becomes the perfect killer, trusted by no one.

### Breaking Points
- Player betrays Sash
- Player uses Sash's skills for personal gain
- Player refuses to help against the network

## 8) Ghor Iron-Root

### Backstory
Ghor was a hunter who was attacked by a bear. The bear's spirit merged with him to save his life. Now he's part of both human and beast, hunted by extremists from both sides. He seeks to prove coexistence is possible.

### Personal Quest: "The Hunt's End"
Monster hunters attack the Choke. Ghor must choose:

**Good Path (Harmony):** Protect both humans and Choke creatures.

**Evil Path (War):** Lead the Choke creatures to wipe out the hunters.

**Quest Subversion:** The hunters were sent by a corrupt Core official who wants to harvest Choke resources. Both Ghor and the hunters are victims.

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Chieftain:** Becomes a leader who bridges both worlds.
- **Warlord:** Leads an army against humanity.

### Breaking Points
- Player sides with the hunters
- Player kills Choke creatures without reason
- Player treats Ghor as a monster

---

## 9) Vek "Glass"

### Backstory
Vek was a brilliant student who experimented with unstable magic. It worked - but his body became crystalline and fragile. He seeks a cure, or if none exists, to make his power worth the sacrifice.

### Personal Quest: "The Glass Heart"
A cure exists - but it's made from the life force of another. Vek must choose:

**Good Path (Sacrifice):** Refuse the cure, use his remaining time to help others.

**Evil Path (Survival):** Take the cure, become fully human again.

**Quest Subversion:** The donor is a dying criminal who volunteers. Is accepting a willing sacrifice wrong?

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Selfless Scholar:** Accepts fate, becomes a legend of knowledge.
- **Fractured God:** Becomes something beyond human.

### Breaking Points
- Player pressures Vek to take the cure
- Player treats Vek as fragile/weapon
- Player refuses to help find a cure

## 10) Maeva Still-Water

### Backstory
Maeva's village was destroyed by "corruption" from the Choke. She survived by bonding with the crystal essence of the mountain streams. She seeks to understand what destroyed her home - and if it can be undone.

### Personal Quest: "Crystal Tears"
The "corruption" was actually pure Choke magic - the village was built on sacred ground. Maeva must choose:

**Good Path (Understanding):** Learn to control the crystal magic, protect others.

**Evil Path (Revenge):** Destroy the mountain's defenses, let chaos reign.

**Quest Subversion:** The corruption is spreading. Maeva's village was a warning. She must decide if stopping it is possible or if evacuation is the only option.

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Crystal Sage:** Masters the crystal magic, becomes a protector.
- **Corrupted Crystal:** Becomes the very thing that destroyed her village.

### Breaking Points
- Player dismisses Maeva's trauma
- Player refuses to investigate the corruption
- Player prioritizes profit over the village

## 11) Void-Exile (Silas)

### Backstory
Silas was once a devoted monk who lost his faith when his prayers went unanswered during a disaster. He fell into darkness - literally - and now walks between light and shadow. He seeks meaning in a meaningless universe.

### Personal Quest: "The Void's Embrace"
Silas discovers his "fall" was engineered - someone wanted him to become a bridge between light and dark. He must choose:

**Good Path (Balance):** Reject being a pawn, forge his own path.

**Evil Path (Power):** Embrace the role, become the ultimate weapon.

**Quest Subversion:** The entity that engineered his transformation is dying. Silas must decide whether to save it or let it die.

### Transformation Outcomes
- **Purified:** Achieves true balance, transcends the void.
- **Shadow-Worn:** Becomes a creature of pure darkness.

### Breaking Points
- Player forces Silas to choose between light and dark
- Player threatens Silas's faith
- Player uses Silas as a weapon
